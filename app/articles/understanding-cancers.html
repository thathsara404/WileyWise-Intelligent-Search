Understanding Cancers

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. If the spread is not controlled, it can result in death. Cancer can develop in almost any tissue or organ in the body.

Common Types of Cancer

  • Breast Cancer
  • Lung Cancer
  • Prostate Cancer
  • Skin Cancer
  • Colon and Rectal Cancer

Causes and Risk Factors

While the exact cause of cancer is not always known, several risk factors can increase the likelihood of developing the disease. These include:

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Smoking and tobacco use
  • Exposure to carcinogens (e.g., radiation, chemicals)
  • Poor diet and lack of physical activity
  • Infections (e.g., HPV, hepatitis B and C)

Symptoms of Cancer

Symptoms vary depending on the type and stage of cancer but may include unexplained weight loss, fatigue, lumps or swelling, and persistent pain.

Prevention and Treatment

Preventative measures include a healthy lifestyle, regular screenings, and vaccinations (e.g., HPV vaccine). Treatments can range from surgery and chemotherapy to radiation therapy and targeted therapies, depending on the type and stage of cancer.
